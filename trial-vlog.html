My First Journey To Shillong

Author: Noob Traveller Imtiaz

Shillong is a hill station and the capital of Meghalaya, a state in northeastern India. It is the headquarters of the East Khasi Hills district. Shillong is the 330th most populous city in India with a population of 143,229 according to the 2011 census.[7] It is said that the rolling hills around the town reminded the British of Scotland. Hence, they would also refer to it as the "Scotland of the East".[8] Shillong has steadily grown in size since it was made the civil station of the Khasi and Jaintia Hills in 1864 by the British. In 1874, on the formation of Assam as the Chief Commissioner's Province, it was chosen as the headquarters of the new administration because of its convenient location between the Brahmaputra and Surma valleys and more so because the climate of Shillong was much cooler than tropical India.[9] Shillong remained the capital of undivided Assam until the creation of the new state of Meghalaya on 21 January 1972, when Shillong became the capital of Meghalaya, and Assam moved its capital to Dispur in Guwahati.

My Second Journey To Debotakhum Bandarban

Author: Noob Traveller Imtiaz

Devtakhum is a place of natural beauty in Bandarban district . Clear water flows here between the high mountains on both sides. It is an attractive place for tourists.Devtakhum is located in Rowangchhari Upazila of Bandarban District.According to the locals of Bandarban it is about 50 feet deep and about 600 feet long. [1] Close to it is Shilbandha Jharna. There are huge forests on both sides of this khum. Because of the steep hills, direct sunlight does not reach inside the khum. So Khum feels cooler the further you go inside. The place is very quiet and noise free. Its water is also very clear. Going inside these khums on a bamboo raft gives the tourists a thrilling feeling, which will easily make anyone fall in love with nature.From Bandarban town you have to come to Rowanchari first. From Rowangchari, permission has to be obtained from Kachchapatli Bazar at Liragaon cantonment. From there you can go to Shilbandha Para by trekking for an hour. Devtakhum is near Shilbandha Para. But before Devtakhum another khum called Pong Su Ang has to be crossed. [1] This Khum trekking route becomes somewhat impassable during monsoons. It is not safe to go here during monsoons or any other time without a guide. If you don't know how to swim, you should wear a life jacket before entering the water.

My Third Journey To Cox's Bazar

Author: Noob Traveller Imtiaz

Cox's Bazar is a city, fishing port, tourism centre, and district headquarters in southeastern Bangladesh. Cox's Bazar Beach, one of the most popular tourist attractions in Bangladesh, is the longest uninterrupted natural beach in the world.[1][2] Located 150 km (93 mi) south of the city of Chittagong, Cox's Bazar is also known by the name Panowa, which translates literally as "yellow flower." An old name was "Palongkee". Kutupalong refugee camp, the world's largest refugee camp, is in Cox's Bazar.[3][4] The city covers an area of 23.4 km2 (9.0 sq mi) with 58 mahallas and 27 wards and as of 2022 had a population of nearly 200,000.[5] Cox's Bazar is connected by road and air with Chittagong.[6][7] The modern Cox's Bazar derives its name from Captain Hiram Cox, an officer of the British East India Company, a Superintendent of Palongkee outpost. To commemorate his role in refugee rehabilitation work, a market was established and named after him. It is one of Bangladesh's main tourist spots. In 2023 Bangladesh evacuated over 50,000 people to safe shelters as Cyclone Mocha approached.[8]
